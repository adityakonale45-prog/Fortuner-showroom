Fortuner

First CLASS

The latest Toyota Fortuner models in late 2025 include the updated Fortuner Leader Edition with cosmetic tweaks, the existing powerful diesel lineup with options like the Legender and GR-S, and recently introduced 48-volt mild-hybrid (Neo Drive) variants for better efficiency, all offering robust 4x2 and 4x4 choices with advanced connectivity features.

Second CLASS

The "middle model" for the Toyota Fortuner often refers to the well-equipped, popular 4x2 Diesel variants with automatic transmission, especially those featuring the new 48V Mild-Hybrid "Neo Drive" system, bridging the gap between base models and the premium Legender/GR-S trims, offering great power, features, and Toyota's reliability without the top-tier price tag

Third CLASS

The first model of the Toyota Fortuner was globally launched in 2004 and came to India in September 2009, based on the robust Hilux platform. This initial generation (2009-2012 in India) featured a 3.0L D-4D turbo diesel engine with 4WD, establishing its reputation as a reliable, rugged, ladder-frame SUV for emerging markets, known for its off-road capability and value retention.
